Threat Scanning Checklist

    When a piece moves:

  1. What does the piece immediately attack?
  2. What squares does the piece attack that are attacked by other pieces (coordination)
  3. By moving the piece what other pieces can now move/attack (discovered attack, development)
  4. Where can the piece move next and what will it attack from each possible subsequent square?

Quick Reference

1
Defensive Options
  1. Mate
  2. Capture the attacker
  3. Capture a piece of greater value
  4. Attack a piece of greater value
  5. Blunder: can they block and counter attack your attacker? (simultaneous attack)
  6. Pin/Skewer the attacker
  7. Defend the attacked piece
  8. Blunder: is the attacked piece a defender? (undercut)
  9. Blunder: is the defender already committed? (draw away)
  10. Move the attacked piece
  11. Interpose a piece
  12. Compensation
2
Attack Options
  1. Mate
  2. Capture
  3. Skewer
  4. Discovered attack while attacking a piece of greater value or undefended
  5. Attack a pinned piece
  6. Attack a trapped piece
  7. Check
  8. Pin a defender
  9. Fork
  10. Undercut (attack a defender)
  11. Block the defender of an attacked piece
    (eg: interpose a pawn)
  12. Draw away
    capture a piece protected by an overloaded defender
  13. Cut off all retreat squares
  14. Do not move a piece to a square on which it can be attacked by a piece of lesser value
  15. Pin
  16. Attack an undefended piece
  17. Overload
    attack 2 pieces protected by 1 defender
  18. Attack a piece of greater value
  19. Draw to
    capture a piece that draws a defender to create a weakness, eg: double pawns, open file
  20. Pile on attack
  21. Align
    threaten discovered attack
  22. Exchange a less for a more active piece
  23. Attack
  24. Cover valued square
  25. Manuever
    Move toward outposts, open diagnals, open rank and files.
